# Contoso HR Policy: Equal Employment, Diversity & Inclusion

## 1. Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO)

Contoso is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate in hiring, compensation, promotion, or termination on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age (40+), disability, genetic information, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other characteristic protected by applicable law.

All hiring managers and interviewers are required to complete annual EEO compliance training. Violations of this policy are subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.

## 2. Prohibited Interview Topics

The following topics are strictly prohibited in interviews and must never appear in interview notes or evaluation rubrics:

- Marital status, family status, or pregnancy plans
- Age or year of graduation (unless directly relevant to a license requirement)
- Religious observance or holidays
- National origin or citizenship status (note: work authorization may be asked)
- Disability status (reasonable accommodations may be discussed only after a conditional offer)
- Arrest records (without conviction) in jurisdictions where restricted
- Credit history (unless the role is finance-sensitive and background check is authorized)

Any candidate who believes a prohibited topic was raised must be directed to HR immediately. Interviewers who raise prohibited topics will be removed from interview panels pending investigation.

## 3. Structured Interviews

All interviews for roles at Level 4 and above must use a structured interview format with pre-approved questions. Structured interviews reduce bias by ensuring all candidates are asked the same questions in the same order.

Interview question sets must be reviewed and approved by HR before use. Ad-hoc questions that deviate from the approved set must be documented with rationale.

## 4. Diverse Slates

Hiring managers are expected to ensure diverse candidate slates. Before extending an offer, the hiring panel must confirm that:
- At least 50% of candidates who received a hiring manager interview were from underrepresented groups (where applicant pool permits), OR
- The talent acquisition team has documented a good-faith effort to source diverse candidates.

This requirement applies to all roles at Level 5 and above.

## 5. Bias Awareness in Resume Screening

Resume screeners must evaluate candidates based on skills, experience, and demonstrated competencies relevant to the role. The following are examples of screening bias that must be avoided:

- **Affinity bias**: Preferring candidates with similar backgrounds to the screener
- **Name bias**: Making assumptions based on candidate names
- **Institution bias**: Automatic preference for graduates of specific universities
- **Recency bias**: Penalizing candidates with non-linear career paths

When in doubt, focus on: relevant skills, demonstrated outcomes, and growth trajectory.

## 6. Reasonable Accommodations

Candidates with disabilities may request reasonable accommodations for any part of the hiring process (e.g., extended assessment time, alternative interview formats). Requests must be directed to HR and processed within 5 business days.

Conditional job offers may include a statement that the offer is contingent upon the company's ability to provide reasonable accommodations if required.

## 7. Pay Equity

All offers must fall within the approved compensation band for the role level. Offers outside the band require VP + HR approval. Compensation decisions must not be based on a candidate's prior salary history in jurisdictions where salary history bans apply.

HR conducts an annual pay equity audit. Results are reviewed by the CHRO and shared with department heads.

## 8. Reporting and Retaliation Prohibition

Employees and candidates may report EEO concerns to HR, their manager's manager, or anonymously via the ethics hotline. Retaliation against anyone who raises an EEO concern in good faith is strictly prohibited and is itself a terminable offense.
